The Leader Who Learned to Focus

Once, there was a young Khalifa named Aaraiz. People looked up to him — he was kind, smart, and full of potential. 1 But whenever someone needed his help, his mind would wander to video games, cartoons, and what he'd eat for dinner. 2

One day, his friend came to him with a problem. Aaraiz tried his best to listen, but his thoughts were everywhere except on his friend's words. 12 He could not help. He could not lead.

That night, Aaraiz sat quietly and remembered something: a leader who cannot focus cannot lead. 3 He thought about the Sahaba — how they would memorize pages of revelation, sit with the Prophet ﷺ, and not miss a single word. 4 Their purpose was clear. Their focus was their superpower. 5

So Aaraiz made a decision. He would honor his time, because time is the most precious gift — and a Khalifa does not waste gifts. 6 He would do things with ihsan — with full attention and excellence. 7 And when someone needed him, he would truly listen, truly understand, and truly serve. 7

Slowly, people noticed the change. Aaraiz wasn't just smart anymore — he was present. And that made all the difference.

Because great things do not happen by accident. They happen when you focus. 6

"You cannot do anything if you can't focus."
