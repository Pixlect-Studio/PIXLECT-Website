Nevermind, What Is Islamabad

Islamabad is the capital city of Pakistan.

Unlike many older, historic cities that grew naturally over centuries, Islamabad is a planned city. It was built from scratch in the 1960s to replace Karachi as the country's capital.

Here is a quick breakdown of what makes it unique:

The Grid Layout: The city is designed in a highly organized grid system divided into sectors (like E-7, F-6, G-9, etc.). Each sector has its own central shopping area (called a markaz) and residential spaces, making it very structured and easy to navigate.

Greenery and Nature: It is famous for being incredibly clean, quiet, and full of trees. It sits right at the edge of the Margalla Hills, which give the city a beautiful mountain backdrop and great hiking trails.

Faisal Mosque: One of its most famous landmarks is the Faisal Mosque. It’s known for its unique, modern design that looks like a Bedouin tent instead of a traditional domed mosque, and it sits right against the hills.

What Is The Most Major Wars

When historians talk about the "most major" wars in human history, they usually look at two things: the global impact (how much it reshaped borders, governments, and technology) and the human cost (the loss of life).

Here are the most significant and devastating wars in history, broken down by era:

🌍 1. The Global World Wars (20th Century)
These conflicts completely reshaped the modern map, pushed industrial technology to its limits, and created the international systems (like the UN) we have today.

World War II (1939–1945): The deadliest conflict in human history. It was an all-out global war between the Allies (US, UK, Soviet Union, etc.) and the Axis powers (Germany, Japan, Italy). It introduced nuclear weapons and resulted in an estimated 70 to 85 million deaths, including millions of civilians in the Holocaust.

What Is CGI

CGI stands for Computer-Generated Imagery.

It refers to any still or animated visual content created using computer software. It can be 2D or 3D, but when people use the term casually today, they are almost always talking about 3D computer graphics used in movies, video games, television, and commercials.

Here is a quick look at how it works and where it is used:

⚙️ How CGI is Made (The Basic Pipeline)
Creating a CGI element isn't just about drawing on a screen; it's an entire digital construction process:

Modeling: Artists build the shape of an object or character out of millions of tiny connected polygons—forming a skeleton-like wireframe.

Texturing: Digital "paint," shaders, and maps are applied to the wireframe to make it look like skin, metal, cloth, or concrete.

Rigging & Animation: Animators build an internal digital skeleton for characters so their limbs, muscles, and faces can move naturally.

Lighting & Rendering: Virtual lights are placed in the digital scene to cast realistic shadows and reflections. The computer then calculates all this mathematical data to output the final pixels (rendering).

🎬 CGI vs. VFX: What’s the Difference?
People often mix these two terms up, but they have distinct meanings in film production:

CGI is the actual asset or imagery created entirely inside the computer (e.g., a 3D model of a dragon, a digital spaceship, or a sci-fi city).

VFX (Visual Effects) is the overall process of combining that CGI with real-world, live-action footage filmed on a camera (e.g., placing that digital dragon so it looks like it's flying over a real mountain range).

🎮 Where Else is it Used?
While movies like Avatar or Marvel films are famous for CGI, it is everywhere:

Video Games: Every modern 3D game uses real-time CGI engines to render environments and characters instantly as you move your controller.

Product Marketing: Most commercial car advertisements or smartphone reveals don't use real cameras anymore. They use hyper-realistic 3D CAD (computer-aided design) models rendered to look absolutely perfect.

Architecture: Architects use CGI to create walkthroughs of buildings before a single brick is laid in the real world.
